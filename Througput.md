There is a famous math equation in performance testing called Little's Law:$Concurrency = \text{Requests Per Second} \times \text{Response Time}$Let's plug your numbers in for the hardest part of the test (4 RPS):Target Speed: 4 RPSServer Wait Time: ~15.4 seconds (from your earlier test)Math: $4 \times 15.4 = \mathbf{61.6 \text{ Threads}}$This means just to maintain 4 RPS, JMeter needed at least 62 threads constantly waiting in the queue. If the server got even slower during the test (e.g., latency spiked to 25 seconds), then $4 \times 25 = 100$.If all 100 of your threads got stuck waiting for the GPU to answer, JMeter physically had 0 threads left to send the next request! The timer wanted to send 4 RPS, but its hands were tied, so the outgoing traffic collapsed.
